# Medical-training disclaimer

A formal statement of what Rohy is and is not. Compliance owners should
treat this page as the scope boundary of the platform.

::: warning
**Rohy is a medical-education and clinical-simulation training tool. It is
not a medical device. It must not be used in the care of, or for decisions
about, real patients.**
:::

## Scope

Rohy presents **virtual patients** — synthetic, authored scenarios with
simulated vitals, simulated laboratory and radiology results, and an
AI-driven patient persona. Every clinical signal a trainee sees is
generated by the scenario engine and authored case content. Nothing in the
system observes, measures, diagnoses, or treats a real human being.

The platform exists to let learners practice history-taking, examination,
ordering, interpretation, and management in a safe, repeatable, instrumented
environment, and to let educators author scenarios and review learner
activity.

## Not a medical device

Rohy:

- is **not** cleared, approved, certified, or registered as a medical
  device by any regulatory body;
- performs **no** diagnosis, triage, or treatment recommendation for real
  patients;
- produces outputs (vitals, lab values, AI patient responses, any
  emotion-capture aggregates) that are **simulated or research signals**,
  not clinical findings;
- makes **no** automated decision about a real learner's competence,
  progression, or grade derived from emotion capture (see
  [Oyon &amp; EU AI Act](/security/oyon-ai-act)).

## No real patient data

::: danger
Do **not** enter real patient identifiable information, real protected
health information, or any data from an actual clinical encounter into
Rohy. The platform is built for synthetic scenario content. It carries no
HIPAA, GDPR-clinical, or medical-device compliance certification, and the
deploying organization is responsible for ensuring only simulated data is
used.
:::

Cases, scenarios, and patient personas are authored teaching content. Any
resemblance to a real individual should be removed before a case is
published.

## Compliance posture, not certification

Rohy implements security and data-handling controls — role-based access
([RBAC](/security/rbac)), a tamper-evident
[audit chain](/security/audit-chain), centralized
[response redaction](/security/redaction), and
[data retention](/security/retention) with purge. These **support** an
organization's own compliance work; they are **not** a substitute for it
and **do not** constitute certification. Rohy holds no FIPS, HIPAA, or
medical-device attestation. Where this documentation describes alignment
with a regulation, it describes posture and design intent, not certified
compliance.

## Responsibility of the deploying organization

The organization deploying Rohy is responsible for:

- restricting use to training with synthetic data only;
- obtaining any legal/ethics sign-off required for optional features (in
  particular Oyon emotion capture under EU AI Act Art. 5 — see
  [Oyon &amp; EU AI Act](/security/oyon-ai-act));
- production hardening (see the [Hardening checklist](/security/hardening));
- communicating to learners and educators that all clinical content is
  simulated.
