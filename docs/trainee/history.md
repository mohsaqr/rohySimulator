# Taking a history

You take the history by chatting with the patient in the **Patient** room.
The patient is driven by a language model and answers in character — ask the
way you would ask a real person.

::: warning Training only
The patient is simulated. Their answers are generated and may be incomplete
or inconsistent. Nothing said here is medical advice.
:::

## Talk to the patient

1. Go to the **Patient** room (the bottom-bar room you start in).
2. Type your question in the message box at the bottom of the chat panel —
   for example `What brought you in today?` or
   `Can you describe the pain?`
3. Press **Enter** or click the send button.

Your **first message starts the session**. There is no separate start
button. The patient's reply streams in as it is generated.

::: tip Ask open questions first
The patient answers what you ask. Open questions ("Tell me about the pain")
usually surface more than closed ones early on; narrow down as you go, just
as in a real history.
:::

## Other people in the room

Some cases include other characters you can speak to — for example a
**nurse**, a **relative**, or an on-call **consultant**. When a case has
them, they appear as extra tabs along the top of the chat panel next to the
patient.

- Click a tab to switch who you are talking to.
- Some characters are not always available. If a character must be summoned,
  use the **Call** button shown on their tab; the message box stays disabled
  with a "not available" note until they arrive.
- Each conversation is kept separately, so switching tabs does not mix up
  who said what.

## Voice or text

By default you type. If your instructor has enabled voice mode, a **Voice**
button appears at the top-right of the chat panel.

- Click **Voice** to switch to talking out loud. The button then reads
  **Voice on**, and the patient speaks their replies.
- In voice mode the patient tab shows a **Click to talk** button. Click it,
  speak, then click again to stop and send.
- Click **Voice on** again to go back to typing.

Full detail, requirements and fallbacks are in [Voice mode](/trainee/voice).

## The transcript

Everything you and the patient say is shown in the chat panel as a running
transcript. In voice mode the transcript is hidden by default for a more
realistic feel; use the **Show** / **Hide** control at the top of the chat
panel to bring it back or tuck it away.

## What gets recorded

Your questions and the patient's answers are part of the session record your
instructor reviews in the debrief and in their reporting. Treat the
conversation as part of your assessed work.
