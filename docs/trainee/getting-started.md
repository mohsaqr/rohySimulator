# Getting started

Run your first virtual-patient case in about ten minutes. This page takes you
from the login screen to a live session with a patient who answers your
questions and a monitor that responds to what you do.

::: warning Training only
The simulated patient is not real and the simulator does not provide medical
advice. Nothing you do here affects a real person, and nothing here is
clinical guidance. See the [glossary](/reference/glossary) for how terms are
used across this documentation.
:::

## Before you start

You need:

- A user account (username and password). If you do not have one, use
  **Create Account** on the sign-in screen, or ask your Teacher for
  credentials.
- A modern browser. Chrome or Edge are recommended, especially if you plan to
  use [voice mode](/trainee/voice).
- The site served over **HTTPS** if you want to talk to the patient with your
  microphone — speech recognition will not start on an insecure origin.

## 1. Sign in

1. Open the simulator. You land on the **Sign In** screen.
2. Enter your **Username** and **Password**.
3. Click **Sign In**.

If sign-in fails, the screen shows the reason in a red banner. Check the
username and password, then try again. To make a new account, click **Create
Account** and follow the register form.

## 2. Pick a case

A **case** is one clinical scenario — a patient with a presentation, labs,
imaging, exam findings and treatments behind it.

- If your instructor set a **default case**, the simulator loads it
  automatically and you can start talking right away.
- To choose a different case, open **Settings** (top-left of the patient
  screen) → **Open Settings**, go to the **Cases** tab, and open the case you
  want. Loading a case returns you to the patient screen with that patient
  ready.

You will not see the diagnosis spoiled in the screen header — that is by
design. You work it out.

## 3. Start the session

There is no separate "Start" button. **Your first message to the patient
starts the session.** The chat panel is on the lower-left of the screen.

1. Type a question in the message box, for example
   `Hello, what brought you in today?`
2. Press **Enter** or click the send button.

The patient replies, the session begins, and the monitor on the right starts
running the patient's vitals. From this point everything you do — questions,
exams, orders, treatments — is part of one **session**.

## 4. Work the case

You now have five **rooms**, switched from the bar along the bottom of the
screen:

| Room | What you do there |
|---|---|
| **Patient** | Take the history by chatting with the patient |
| **Examination** | Perform a physical exam on a body map |
| **Laboratory** | Order and read blood and other lab tests |
| **Radiology** | Order and read imaging |
| **Consultant** | Debrief and reflect after you end the case |

See [the five rooms](/trainee/rooms) for how moving between them works.
Order medications from the **Treatments** button that floats above the bottom
bar on the patient screen.

## 5. End and debrief

When you are finished:

1. On the patient screen, click **End & Debrief** (top-right).
2. Confirm in the dialog. Ending the case stops the patient's timeline and
   locks orders, exams and chat.
3. You land in the **Consultant** room to review and reflect. You cannot
   reopen an ended case.

::: tip Refresh is safe
Refreshing the page or closing the tab does **not** end your session. The
simulator restores your case, your room and your work when you come back. A
session ends only when you click **End & Debrief**, switch to a different
case, or your instructor ends it. See the
[FAQ](/trainee/faq) if a session looks lost.
:::
